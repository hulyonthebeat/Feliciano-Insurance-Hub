Protecting Your Las Vegas Home This Summer

Summer in Las Vegas means sunshine, pool days, and triple-digit temperatures, but it can also bring unique risks for homeowners. From extreme heat to monsoon storms, taking a few preventative steps now can help protect your home, save money, and avoid costly insurance claims later.

Prepare for Extreme Heat

Las Vegas summers are no joke. Prolonged heat can put stress on your roof, air conditioning system, and even plumbing. Make sure your AC is serviced before temperatures peak, replace air filters regularly, and inspect your roof for cracked tiles or signs of wear.

It’s also a good idea to check weather stripping around doors and windows to help keep cool air inside and energy bills lower.

Be Ready for Monsoon Season

Summer storms in Southern Nevada can arrive quickly with strong winds, heavy rain, and flash flooding. Clean out gutters and drains to prevent water buildup, and trim any trees or branches that could fall during high winds.

If you live in an area prone to flooding, review your insurance coverage to understand what is and isn’t covered before storm season begins.

Protect Your Home While Traveling

Many families travel during the summer months, making homes more vulnerable to theft. Consider installing smart security cameras, motion lights, or a monitored alarm system. Ask a trusted neighbor to collect packages or check on your property while you’re away.

Simple steps like putting lights on timers and holding mail delivery can also make your home appear occupied.

Reduce Fire Risks

Outdoor grills, dry landscaping, and extreme heat can increase fire hazards during the summer. Keep grills away from structures, avoid storing flammable items in garages exposed to heat, and clear dry brush or debris around your property.

Checking smoke detectors and replacing batteries is another quick but important safety measure.
