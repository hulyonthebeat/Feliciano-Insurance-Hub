What Insurance Does a Small Business Need? A Complete Guide for Business Owners

What Insurance Does a Small Business Need? A Complete Guide for Business Owners

Starting and running a small business is exciting, but it also comes with risks. From unexpected accidents to legal claims, even a single incident can have a major financial impact. That’s why having the right insurance coverage isn’t just smart, it’s essential.

At Feliciano Jiron Insurance Agency, we help small business owners find the right protection tailored to their unique needs. Here’s a breakdown of the most important types of insurance every small business should consider.

1. General Liability Insurance

This is the foundation of most business insurance plans. General liability insurance protects your business from claims involving:

  • Bodily injury (like a customer slipping in your store)
  • Property damage
  • Personal or advertising injury (such as defamation)

Without it, you could be responsible for legal fees, medical costs, and settlements out of pocket.

2. Professional Liability Insurance (Errors & Omissions)

If your business provides services or advice, this coverage is crucial. It protects against claims of:

  • Negligence
  • Mistakes
  • Failure to deliver services as promised

Even if you didn’t do anything wrong, legal defense costs alone can be expensive.

3. Commercial Property Insurance

Whether you own or lease your space, this insurance covers damage to:

  • Buildings
  • Equipment
  • Inventory
  • Furniture

Events like fires, theft, or natural disasters can disrupt your business. This coverage helps you recover quickly.

4. Workers’ Compensation Insurance

If you have employees, most states require this coverage. It provides:

  • Medical benefits for work-related injuries
  • Wage replacement for injured employees
  • Protection for employers against lawsuits

It’s not just a legal requirement, it shows your team you value their safety.

5. Business Owner’s Policy (BOP)

A Business Owner’s Policy bundles key coverages (typically general liability and commercial property) into one cost-effective package. It’s a great option for small to mid-sized businesses looking for comprehensive protection at a lower price.

6. Commercial Auto Insurance

If your business uses vehicles for operations, personal auto policies won’t cover business-related incidents. Commercial auto insurance protects against:

  • Accidents
  • Vehicle damage
  • Liability claims

7. Cyber Liability Insurance

In today’s digital world, even small businesses are targets for cyberattacks. This coverage helps with:

  • Data breaches
  • Customer notification costs
  • Legal fees
  • Reputation management

If you store customer information, this is increasingly important.

8. Business Interruption Insurance

What happens if your business has to temporarily close due to a disaster? Business interruption insurance helps cover:

  • Lost income
  • Operating expenses
  • Rent and payroll

It can be the difference between reopening and shutting down permanently.

Every Business Is Different. Your Coverage Should Be Too.

There’s no one-size-fits-all insurance plan. The type of coverage you need depends on your industry, size, location, and risk level.
